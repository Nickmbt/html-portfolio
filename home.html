A "movie series"

refers to a collection of related films that share the same fictional universe or are marketed as a series, essentially meaning multiple movies that follow the same storyline and characters, often released in succession. Key points about movie series:

Shared universe:

The most defining feature is that the movies within a series take place in the same fictional world with interconnected characters and plot elements.

Sequels and prequels:

Most movie series consist of sequels (films following the original story) and sometimes prequels (films set before the original story).

Franchise:

Often, a movie series is considered a "franchise" which means it is marketed and managed as a single brand, encompassing not just the films but also merchandise, video games, and other related media.

Examples of popular movie series:


  1. Star Wars
  2. The Lord of the Rings
  3. The Godfather
  4. Harry Potter
  5. Marvel Cinematic Universe
  6. James Bond
  7. The Fast and the Furious
